Target Audience Survey

I created a Google Forms survey to learn more about the AI Fairytales project's possible audience. The objective was to learn more about parents' and caregivers' requirements, preferences, and expectations with relation to interactive, artificial intelligence-generated storytelling for kids.

- Situation: To make sure the product will satisfy the demands of the stakeholders, my team wanted input from the target audience.

- Task: In order to gather information on important topics including storytelling preferences, the value of educational materials, and desired interface, I was given the task to put together and distribute a survey.

- Action: I created a brief, easy-to-use survey and distributed it to parents and caregivers directly as well as on social media. The questions centered on their expectations for interactions with children, their interest in AI-generated content, and how frequently they utilize digital storytelling tools. In order to better comprehend the survey's flow and eliminate unnecessary questions, this survey was later recreated with the use of a flowchart.

- Result: Although the survey produced useful early perspectives, its statistical reliability was limited due to the small number of replies. In spite of this, the data provided helpful advice for adjusting the project's course, emphasizing important concepts like the significance of educational value and user-friendly design.

- Reflection: The result reaffirmed the importance of using a bigger sample size in studies to guarantee reliable results. But it also showed the value even small-scale surveys have in determining creative direction, offering crucial early feedback that would have affected the progress of our project. (Had i not switched semesters of course.)

Blik Theatre Stylescape

The Blik Theatre's stylescape attempted to portray the essence of the theater by combining professionalism, emotion, and playfulness into a cohesive brand identity.

- Situation: Blik Theatre required an original rebranding, or maybe just some branding in general, that mirrored its function as a playback theatre for public performances and team-building exercises.

- Task: I had to design a stylescape that reflected the theater's basic principles of inclusivity, creativity, and connection.

- Action: I looked at color schemes, typography, and imagery through my research. I incorporated human-focused graphics to emphasize narrative and teamwork, and I used strong typefaces and warm, brilliant colors to communicate liveliness and connection.

- Result: My finished stylescape was well received within the team, but we ended up choosing someone else's stylescape by vote.

- Reflection: This assignment strengthened my ability to communicate emotions visually and the ability to match a brand's emotional core with its design.

Blik Theatre Moodboard

Brandguide Patched Studio

My classmates and I worked together to design this brand guide for Patched Studio. It creates the visual identity of our studio and guarantees a cohesive, modern aesthetic throughout the branding. My biggest contributions were centered on button design elements, standards for logo usage of the logo's we designed, and telling my members about modern design standards like rounded edges. Through voting and feedback, I also contributed to the final color scheme selection.

- Situation: To establish our identity, our design studio required a complete brand guide that included design guidelines, typography, color schemes, and logos.

- Task: My tasks were establishing standards for logo usage, designing modern button components, and making sure rounded edges were applied consistently. I also offered suggestions while the color scheme was being chosen.

- Action:

  • Logo Misuse: Clearly defined guidelines were created on the proper use of the logo, including limitations on placement, monochromatic usage, and background color choices.
  • Design Elements: To guarantee a modern, clean user interface with rounded edges, i made button styles, including ghost buttons.
  • Color Palette Feedback: I took part in the selection process by looking over, evaluating, and voting on several palettes inspired by the dark theme of Visual Studio Code before settling on the current one.

- Result: Patched Studio's visual identity was successfully established by the brand guide, which also got good reviews from team members and teachers. My work made sure the brand kept a consistent, polished appearance and followed modern design trends.

- Reflection: The importance of attention to detail in brand design was shown by this task. I learned how crucial it is to strike a balance between creative input and design principles, which resulted in an effective and well-received brand guide.

Prototype Fix That UX Zekur

I worked with two students to redesign the ZEKUR website as part of the "Fix That UX" assignment. Our goal was to make the process of getting health insurance easier to navigate and more efficient. I produced a working prototype by incorporating my ideas from my wireframes, including tables and more coherent information, into their wireframes. Because I was capable of successfully mixing design and usability improvements, this project demonstrates my proficiency with interactive media goods.

- Situation: There were a number of issues with the ZEKUR website, such as difficult navigation and a complicated health insurance registration procedure. In order to improve the user experience, we began to revamp the website, paying attention to navigation and streamlining the sign-up procedure.

- Task: Two of my classmates and I worked together to develop a prototype that solved these issues. It was my duty to offer suggestions, improve the design, and make sure the prototype complied with user requirements. To increase usability and clarity, I also included components like tables and other information.

- Action: We began by analyzing the wireframes that my teammates had already produced. I helped by making recommendations for improvements including utilizing tables to properly display information and including a progress bar for the sign-up process. Once the design was complete, we later used HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to create a website out of this prototype that accurately represented the finished idea. I also set up a usertest to see how well our transformation worked.

- Result: The stated pain points were successfully dealt with thanks to the prototype. The process of signing up for health insurance was made easier to navigate and more aesthetically pleasing. The findings of the usertest showed that users also found the website easier to use.

- Reflection: The importance of teamwork and communication when creating successful interactive media products has been proven by this task. My group members and I were able to produce a well-rounded prototype by combining our thoughts.

The Prototype

Usertest Fix That UX Prototype

I tested our updated ZEKUR website prototype with a young adult who was interested in health insurance for the first time in order to gauge its efficiency. Finding any usability problems and making sure the prototype fulfilled user expectations were the objectives.

- Situation: Our prototype targeted young adults as one of the main user groups and sought to streamline ZEKUR's health insurance sign-up procedure. Testing the design and flow's intuitiveness for someone who was not familiar with health insurance procedures was crucial.

- Task:To find areas for improvement, I had to evaluate the website's usability, see how a user interacted with it, and get feedback. I was able to examine their navigational patterns and identify any problems by recording the session.

- Action:I invited an possible end user to utilize the prototype to sign up for health insurance during the test. They chose a package after navigating through the pages and utilizing the features. To make sure all of the observations were noted, the session was recorded. According to their comments, I saw that although the content and navigation were understandable, the labels on the "Continue" button varied from page to page, occasionally saying "Continue," "Next," or "Go Further." I quickly made changes to the prototype to improve clarity by using consistent button labeling.

- Result: The website was straightforward to use, and the packages were detailed enough for the end user to make an informed choice. No significant usability issues were found, and the test was successful aside from the button label issue. The consistency and effectiveness of the prototype have been improved by the changes made from the usertest.

- Reflection:The significance of minute details in user experience design was emphasized by this usertest. Even though the design worked well overall, fixing small errors like button labels can greatly improve user satisfaction. I will make sure that consistency is a priority in my designs going forwards.
